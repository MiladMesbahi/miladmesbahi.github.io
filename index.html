Technical Projects

Battle Bot

April 2022 - June 2022

In this project, we engineered a 1-pound combat robot, focusing on the optimal balance between durability and agility. Our design centered on a precise strength-to-weight ratio, utilizing high-quality aluminum to reduce weight while maintaining structural integrity. The robot's standout feature was an "egg beater" style weapon, strategically designed to undercut opponents effectively. This mechanism accounted for roughly 30% of the robot's total mass, aligning with our goal of maximizing impact while preserving maneuverability. Despite the robot being manually controlled via Bluetooth, considerable attention was given to the electronic setup and protection, ensuring functionality in intense combat scenarios. We leveraged Finite Element Method (FEM) analyses to identify and reinforce structural weak points, leading to iterative design improvements. Our compact and strategically designed robot achieved a notable second place in a highly competitive battle, showcasing the effectiveness of our engineering and design choices.

Autonomous Rover

January 2022 - March 2022

In my first hands-on robotics venture, I designed and built an autonomous rover capable of navigating an obstacle course. Utilizing SolidWorks, I developed a robust chassis to house all electronic components, motors, and sensors efficiently. A key feature was the innovative grabber mechanism equipped with rubberized clampers for object retrieval. The project extensively employed 3D printing technology, utilizing Prusa slicer software and UCLA's facilities for creating parts like motor mounts and battery pack holders. I also spearheaded the programming aspect, optimizing Arduino code to enable intelligent navigation and decision-making for the rover. This project highlights my skills in mechanical design, 3D modeling, rapid prototyping, and programming within the interdisciplinary field of robotics.
