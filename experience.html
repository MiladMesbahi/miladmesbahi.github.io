Mechanical Engineering Internship (Systems Testing)

Jet Propulsion Laboratory - NASA/June 2023 - September 2023

My summer long internship concentrated on various different tasks within the Dynamics and Environments Group at JPL. My main project was focused on evaluating chainmail solids (CMS) as a novel material for damping and shock mitigation in aerospace applications. We aimed to harness the unique properties of the CMS, such as low density and adaptable stiffness, for effective vibration management. Our experimental procedure included a meticulous design of a Data Acquisition System and strategic placement of accelerometers to capture vibrational responses. The chainmail was subjected to random vibration profiles, and the results were analyzed using Power Spectral Density (PSD) comparisons and other modal analysis tools. Significant findings included a staggering -20dB reduction in transmissibility at critical lower frequencies and up to a -30dB reduction at higher, shock-level ranges, indicating the chainmail's effective damping capabilities. Finite Element Method (FEM) simulations complemented our experimental observations, affirming the potential of CMS in aerospace applications. The project's future focus will delve deeper into the chainmail's thermal properties and further tuning for specific aerospace applications, which we explored breifly through its implementation with cryocoolers.
