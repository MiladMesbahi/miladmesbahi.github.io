Technical Projects

Mechanical Engineering Internship (Systems Testing)

Jet Propulsion Laboratory - NASA/June 2023 - September 2023

My summer long internship concentrated on various different tasks within the Dynamics and Environments Group at JPL. My main project was focused on evaluating chainmail solids (CMS) as a novel material for damping and shock mitigation in aerospace applications. We aimed to harness the unique properties of the CMS, such as low density and adaptable stiffness, for effective vibration management. Our experimental procedure included a meticulous design of a Data Acquisition System and strategic placement of accelerometers to capture vibrational responses. The chainmail was subjected to random vibration profiles, and the results were analyzed using Power Spectral Density (PSD) comparisons and other modal analysis tools. Significant findings included a staggering -20dB reduction in transmissibility at critical lower frequencies and up to a -30dB reduction at higher, shock-level ranges, indicating the chainmail's effective damping capabilities. Finite Element Method (FEM) simulations complemented our experimental observations, affirming the potential of CMS in aerospace applications. The project's future focus will delve deeper into the chainmail's thermal properties and further tuning for specific aerospace applications, which we explored breifly through its implementation with cryocoolers. Undergraduate Mechanical Engineering Researcher (Fluid Dynamics)

Thermoscience Laboratory - UCLA/October 2022 - Present

As the lead researcher at UCLA's Thermosciences Lab, my focus is on the detailed study of liquid desiccants and their application in fluid dynamics. My contributions include conducting extensive experiments to explore fluid and string properties, combining hands-on experimental data with simulation insights, as well as performing data analysis through MatLab. I've played a key role in engineering and fabricating custom parts and procedures, significantly enhancing the precision of our fluid dynamics research. Additionally, I've worked on developing and refining MATLAB-based image processing algorithms, using the Image Processing Toolbox to automate the analysis of our liquid droplets under investigation and string curvature under varying tensions. These efforts not only streamline our data analysis process but also strengthen the lab's research capacity, paving the way for upcoming publications.
